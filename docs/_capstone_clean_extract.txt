INTRODUCTION
Project Context
Businesses are switching to digital solutions to increase productivity, improve customer experience, and obtain a competitive edge, which has resulted in substantial changes in the furniture sector. Businesses who don't follow the newest trends in this fast-paced industry run the danger of losing clients to rivals that provide smooth web-based experiences. A number of obstacles impede the expansion and operational effectiveness of Design Excellence, a home and office furniture firm.
One of the most significant issues encountered by Design Excellence Home & Office System Co. is its outdated website. The current website was built using Wix, a accessible website-building but does not fully meet the company’s specific needs. The website lacks a well-organized and user-friendly layout, especially when accessed on smartphones, making it less accessible and unusable for potential customers. Additionally, the company is paying for the platform but has limited control over editing and updating its content, leading to outdated product descriptions and inaccurate information. This lack of control negatively impacts customer trust and the company’s credibility.
The company's inventory management is another significant problem. At the moment, Design Excellence uses Microsoft Excel to manage supply, keep an eye on stock levels, and manage the procurement process. This manual method is ineffective for real-time tracking, sluggish, and prone to mistakes. The lack of an automated system leads to order fulfilment bottlenecks, which raises the possibility of raw material mishandling, delays, and stock shortages. Productivity and customer satisfaction are directly impacted by these operational inefficiencies.
The proponents will be creating DesignXcel, an integrated web-based solution made to specifically fulfill the requirements of Design Excellence, in order to overcome these issues. In addition to improving the company's online visibility, this system will combine essential business functions to optimize operations. All users will have the best possible experience thanks to the new platform's CMS (Content Management System). Consumers will find it easy to choose from a variety of furniture selections, color selections, and make quotations.
DesignXcel will also include an innovative inventory management system that will automate stock tracking and allow for real-time monitoring. Order fulfilment and procurement procedures will be enhanced and mistakes will be decreased as a result. A committed staff member will be on hand to answer questions in real time, guaranteeing seamless communication and increased client involvement in order to improve customer service. To make it simpler for companies to buy office furniture in bulk.
An Order Tracker that lets users track their purchases and see projected delivery times will be included in DesignXcel for more transparency. In order to provide flexibility, the platform will accept a number of payment options, such as cash on-delivery, e-wallets, and bank transfers.
In addition to overcoming its current obstacles, Design Excellence will enhance customer happiness, operational effectiveness, and business expansion by putting DesignXcel into practice. By using contemporary digital technologies, the business will be positioned to compete in the office furniture sector, guaranteeing its viability and success in the changing market.
Purpose and Description of the Project
The goal of this project is to develop DesignXcel, a web-based system designed to enhance the online presence and operational efficiency of Design Excellence. This system will improve website functionality, simplify inventory management, and enhance customer service by providing real-time communication and order tracking.
What makes this project unique is its combination of an interactive content management system and an automated inventory management system. Additionally, the inclusion of a dedicated support feature ensures that customer inquiries are addressed promptly, leading to a smoother and more satisfying shopping experience.
Objectives of the Study
General Objective
The objective of this study is to design and develop DesignXcel, a fully functional web-based system aimed at enhancing the digital presence of Design Excellence. The system seeks to provide a user-friendly, mobile-responsive, and efficient online platform that improves product visibility, customer engagement, as well as automate the inventory management, make a CMS feature to make website editing easier without prior technical expertise and overall business operations.
Specific Objective
Specifically, this study aims to:
Develop a user-friendly and mobile-responsive e-commerce website that provides accurate and up-to-date product listings to ensure a seamless browsing and shopping experience for customers.
Implement an automated inventory management system that enables real-time stock monitoring, minimizes errors, and streamlines procurement and order fulfillment processes.
Include a content management system to the system for managing product listings, updating descriptions, and adjusting prices. An easy-to-use interface for modifying website content without needing technical expertise.
Integrate real-time communication features that allow customers to interact directly with customer service representatives and receive transparent updates on order progress.
Scope and Limitations of the Study
Scope of the project:
The purpose of this study is to design and develop DesignXcel, a web-based system to enhance the digital presence and operational efficiency of Design Excellence. The target users of the system are Design Excellence employees, individual customers looking to purchase office furniture, and business customers seeking bulk orders. The study will focus on the period from development to deployment, testing, and evaluation of the system's impact on business operations.
The features of the proposed system include:
E-commerce website
Product variant and color selections showcase: The ability of the system to view the in-depth details and select product variants and colors.
Customer Inquiry and Real-Time Support: A real-time communication feature for customer support, allowing customers to interact with a dedicated representative.
Payment Options: The system only accepts e-wallets as of now.
Bulk Orders: Allows customers to purchase large quantities of products in a single transaction.
Inventory Management
Content Management System (CMS): Admin dashboard for updating product details and uploading visuals through an easy-to-use interface.
Automated Inventory Management System: An automated inventory management system to enable real-time tracking of stock levels and order fulfilment.
User Role Management: Access control for different system users (e.g., inventory manager, transaction manager, user manager).
Activity logs: Ability to check user activities like changes in the inventory and who changed the inventory.
Sales report: Summarizes transactions and sales performance over specific periods to help the company track trends and revenue.
Limitations:
Internet-dependent platform: The system requires continuous and stable internet connectivity for users and administrators to access product listings, place orders, manage inventory, or update the CMS. Any interruption in connection prevents system usage.
Mobile application not supported: DesignXcel is a responsive web application and does not include a native mobile app; functionality and layout may vary by browser and device.
Manual verification of newly added content: Uploaded products, images, and promotional content must still be manually reviewed and approved by an administrator before going live, which may delay publishing.
Single-language interface: All system text, product information, and interface elements are presented only in English. Support for additional languages or localization is not available.
Single-currency payment processing: The platform only supports Philippine Peso (PHP) for product purchases. Users from other regions cannot view or pay using alternative currencies.
Site-visit project handling: Large or custom orders that necessitate on-site measurements and formal contracts fall outside the scope of the online system and must be managed offline.
External payment gateway dependency: Payment processing relies on third-party digital wallet providers. Any downtime, delays, or issues from these providers directly affect the system's checkout process.

This section outlines the critical components of the system design, detailing how the platform will operate, and the methodologies applied to achieve the project goals.

Figure 1. Data flow diagram
The Data Flow Diagram (DFD) illustrates how data moves between customers, administrative employees, and various processes within the system. Customers can browse products freely but must log in or register before placing an order. The system manages user credentials and profile data through the Account Management process and provides real-time assistance via Chat Support with automated FAQs. Once logged in, customers can add products to their cart, proceed to checkout, and update personal information. Completed orders are stored in the Order Management process, where customers can track, cancel, or confirm receipt of orders and later submit product reviews or ratings.
On the administrative side, employees handle Product and Inventory Management, including adding, editing, and deactivating products, managing raw materials, and ensuring stock levels reflect customer activity. The Order Management process allows administrators to monitor and update order statuses, while the Content Management System (CMS) enables customization of website design, automated responses, and review moderation. All actions performed by employees are recorded in the Activity Logs, ensuring transparency and accountability within the system’s operations.
Figure 2. Work Breakdown Structure (WBS)
This work breakdown structure (WBS) outlines the core components of DesignXcel, a web-based e-commerce and inventory management system integrated with a content management system for Design Excellence Home and Office System Company. The system is divided into four main modules: Chat Support, which enables automated responses and real-time messaging; Customer Interface, which manages user access, product browsing, inquiries, project galleries, shopping cart, and policies; Admin Interface, which provides administrative tools for dashboard monitoring, sales reporting, inventory control, transaction management, user administration, content management, and activity logs; and Ordering, which handles customer details and purchase histories. Together, these components ensure efficient customer interaction, streamlined operations, and effective content and inventory management.
Development
The development of DesignXcel utilized the Waterfall Model as its software development methodology. This approach was chosen because it follows a structured and sequential process where each phase of the project is completed before moving on to the next. Through this model, the team was able to maintain clear documentation, defined goals, and an organized flow of work throughout the development process. It was found to be appropriate for the DesignXcel project since the system requirements were already well defined at the start, and only minimal changes were anticipated during implementation. The Waterfall method allowed the researchers to concentrate on creating a stable and fully functional platform that combines e-commerce features, automated inventory management, and a content management system. This ensured that all project objectives were achieved with accuracy, efficiency, and consistency.
For the design of the software system for Design Excellence Home and Office System Company, the approach must follow the standard practices to ensure a robust, scalable, and user-friendly e-commerce website and inventory management system. The development phase is divided into three core activities:
Database Creation & Population – The team designs the database structure, sets relationships, and fills it with test data to ensure efficient and reliable storage.
Back-End Coding/Programming – Server-side logic, APIs, and integrations are coded with proper security and validation to ensure smooth system performance.
Front-End Development – The user interface is designed and built for responsiveness, interactivity, and accessibility across devices.

CONCLUSION
The development of DesignXcel successfully addressed the operational challenges faced by Design Excellence Home and Office System Company through the integration of a responsive e-commerce website, an automated inventory management system, and a dynamic content management system (CMS). The project achieved its objectives of enhancing the company’s digital presence, improving inventory accuracy, and streamlining customer interactions. By consolidating key business functions into a single web-based platform, the system improved efficiency, minimized manual errors, and provided a more seamless experience for both customers and administrators.
Evaluation results demonstrated that the system performed effectively in terms of functionality, availability, accessibility, and user satisfaction. Users expressed a high level of agreement that DesignXcel was reliable, responsive, and easy to navigate. The automated inventory feature ensured accurate, real-time stock monitoring, while the e-commerce component simplified product browsing and purchasing processes. Additionally, the CMS empowered administrators to manage and update content efficiently without requiring technical expertise, resulting in a platform that adapts quickly to business needs and market changes.
The implementation of DesignXcel represents a meaningful step toward digital transformation within the company. By integrating automation, user-centered design, and responsive technology, the system redefined how the business manages its operations and interacts with customers. This transformation reflects a broader shift from manual processes to intelligent, data-driven solutions, creating a more efficient and customer-focused environment. The system’s coherence between design and functionality not only enhances operational productivity but also reinforces brand credibility and customer trust through improved accessibility and reliability.
To sustain and enhance the platform’s performance, several improvements are recommended. Future development may include expanding payment integrations to support multiple currencies and digital wallets, implementing analytics dashboards to track business performance, and optimizing mobile responsiveness for wider device compatibility. Additionally, incorporating adaptive content features and marketing automation can further strengthen user engagement and administrative flexibility.
In conclusion, DesignXcel proved to be an effective and innovative solution that streamlined Design Excellence’s business processes, elevated customer experience, and modernized its overall operations. By merging technology, functionality, and usability into a unified platform, the system not only fulfilled its objectives but also positioned the company for long-term growth in the digital marketplace. With continuous enhancement and integration of advanced features, DesignXcel holds strong potential to serve as a sustainable and scalable model for digital transformation in the furniture and retail industries.
